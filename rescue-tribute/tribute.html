History of the Durham Rescue Mission

  • 1974 - Dr. Ernie Mills and Mrs. Gail Mills founded the mission out of a small, two-story house on the corner of North Holman and East Main Street in Durham, NC
  • 1978 - A larger church building was obtained to act as a men's shelter just a block away from the original location with space to provide for up to 35 men
  • 1993 - Then a three-story home was opened as a women and children's shelter with capacity for up to five mothers with small children
  • 1997 - To further support need, a new shelter was opened that was able to home 5 families
  • 1998 - Filling in the last gap, a single women's shelter was opened
  • 2006 - The first Durham Rescue Mission thrift store was opened on Highway 55 in Durham, NC
  • 2009 - Another thrift store was opened in Raleigh, NC
  • 2012 - The Chapel Hill thrift store was opened
  • 2013 - The Center For Hope opened its doors offering a 250 seat dining and kitchen facility, educational facilities for the Victory Program with a GED program and computer lab, as well as 3 dormitories.
  • 2015 - A job training and Donation center was opened, and the original Church facility was renovated including a handicap-accessible elevator.
  • 2017 - The Ball Family Student Housing was opened with 66 beds, a family room, and a computer classroom
  • 2018 - A Wake Forest thrift store opened its doors
  • 2019 - The Clegg Family Student Housing was opened with 54 beds, a kitchen, dining room, and TV room
  • 2021 - The Roxboro thift store was opened
  • 2021 - Dr. Ernie Mills and Mrs. Gail Mills stepped away from day to day operations to focus on the Mission's Rescue Legacy Fund, but the mission lives on under the leadership of the new CEO-President Rob Tart

The Victory Program

Restoring Lives Together

The Victory Program is the Durham Rescue Mission’s 12-month holistic program designed to help people overcome a lifestyle of addictions and return to the workforce investing back into the community.

Helping others overcome addictions has always been a priority for the ministry of the Durham Rescue Mission. Dr. Ernie Mills’ personal experience with the alcohol addiction of his father led him to Durham in 1973 with a hope of reaching the addicted. Today, the Victory Program helps restore the lives of dozens of men and women throughout the Triangle suffering from alcohol addiction and drug addiction.
